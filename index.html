Literature Servey

For our literature review, we mainly referred to previously published research papers and journal articles. There is research done on COVID-19 and Pneumonia identification based on Chest X-ray or CT scan images. Therefore, a literature review for this topic was done by referring to some of those research papers.

The research done on similar topics discuss common issues like data overfitting, low accuracy. Previous work they used a small number of datasets, as a result, the probability of the developed systems becoming over-fitting is exceptionally high.

In our work, more data is employed, resulting in greater accuracy than in other related projects. As a result, there is minimal risk of over-fitting the method created here.

Research Gap

There exists a vast gap between the existing apps and the developed application of ours. We have compared some existing mobile applications to seek the main gaps compared with our developed mobile application.

Research Problem

Apart from the significant macroeconomic repercussions, the continuing coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic has resulted in a global health and healthcare disaster. During this pandemic we observed that delay of RT-PCR test is a common issue. Because it should be test in a laboratory by experts and it needs more time. These laboratory tests require lots of resources such as special machines to test samples and it requires RT-PCR test special kits to collect samples from people.

In developing countries such as Sri Lanka, we are dealing with a global pandemic with limited resources and fewer physicians. To contain this outbreak, we must identify COVID-19 infected patients early and isolate them from their close contacts.

To overcome this situation, chest X-ray (CXR) becomes the most time and cost-effective method. Chest X-rays are widely available. In addition, the equipment required for these Chest X-rays is cheap and portable.
